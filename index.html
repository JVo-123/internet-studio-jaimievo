Part 2 of 4: Conversations with Robots

  1. What is the difference between structured content and semantic HTML?

    2. Structured content defines/labels the elements that are being displayed, while semantic HTML is what gives the content meaning to the webpage. The structured content is kind of like the exposition, rising action, climax, and conclusion of a plot; it is the label to each section. The semantic HTML, on the other hand, are instructions that are legible to the webpage the content is on.

  2. How do they work together?

    3. The semantic HTML translates the structured content into markup that the webpage will understand. Structured content is kind of like a cooking recipe, and semantic HTML is like the ingredients that are needed to make the food.

  3. Why is it important to have structured content and semantic HTML in your web pages?

    4. Structured content is needed for organization purposes; otherwise, the semantic HTML would be difficult for people to follow, even while completed. Semantic HTML is important because it’s what webpages use in order to present the content properly. Without one or the other, it could be confusing for humans and algorithms to understand.

  1. Denise Jacobs argues that we all have a troll-like inner critic. Of the methods she identifies to banish this troll, which would be most successful for you? Why?

    2. I think “Go for ‘Good’ Instead of Superlative” would be the best for me.   2. In David McRaney's article, what is "hyperbolic discounting"? List one method McRaney identifies to counteract hyperbolic discounting. Which would work best for you?

    3. Hyperbolic discounting is “the tendency to get more rational when you are forced to wait”, or in other words: focus on the more present or in-reach rewards instead of the rewards in the future. A method that McRaney identifies is accepting that the me right now is more trustworthy than the future me because I know my own state of mind now, but I’m not so sure about the future me. If I wait for future me to arrive, future me could very well be like now me, and end up pushing back tasks further into the future.
